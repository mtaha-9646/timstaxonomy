TIMS Taxonomy

A Modern Framework for Effective Learning

The TIMS Taxonomy provides a structured approach to learning that emphasizes task understanding, information gathering, meaning-making, and knowledge sharing. Designed for modern education, it guides learners through a comprehensive cognitive journey.

The TIMS Taxonomy

Click on each letter to explore the four stages of learning

T
TRY

Try

Reflect, review, and practice to build confidence. Develop resilience and perseverance through rehearsal, preparation, and consolidation of new concepts.

I
IMPROVE

Improve

Make mistakes, ask questions, and design solutions. Challenge your understanding through curiosity, problem-solving, and connecting concepts to real-world applications.

M
MEASURE

Measure

Assess yourself and peers using WAGOLL models. Track progress through feedback loops, respond to input, and validate your understanding against benchmarks.

S
STRETCH

Stretch

Push boundaries through research and innovation. Take creative risks, challenge assumptions, and teach peers while demonstrating tenacity and self-belief.

Framework Creator

Honoring the Visionary

Dr. Timothy John Hughes

Creator of the TIMS Taxonomy

Dr. Timothy John Hughes dedicated his career to understanding how people learn most effectively. In 2015, he introduced the TIMS Taxonomy, a revolutionary framework that emphasizes the interconnected nature of task understanding, information processing, meaning creation, and knowledge sharing.

His work has transformed educational practices worldwide, providing educators with a practical and comprehensive approach to designing learning experiences that truly engage students and promote deep understanding.

2010

Initial Research

Began studying cognitive learning patterns in modern education

2015

TIMS Framework Published

Introduced the TIMS Taxonomy to the educational community

2020

Global Adoption

Framework adopted by educational institutions in 50+ countries

"True learning is not just about acquiring information—it's about understanding the task, gathering knowledge, making meaning, and sharing insights with others. This cycle creates lasting understanding."
